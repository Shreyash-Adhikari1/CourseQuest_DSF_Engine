# Artificial Intelligence Pathway
## BSc (Hons) Computer Science with Artificial Intelligence

---

## Course Overview

This pathway combines a solid foundation in computer science with progressive specialization in artificial intelligence and intelligent systems. Students begin with core computing fundamentals — programming, algorithms, data structures, computer systems, and mathematics — before advancing into machine learning, neural networks, robotics, intelligent agents, natural language processing, and data science.

The program is structured across three years, with each year building directly on the previous. By the final year, students are working on applied machine learning, artificial neural networks, and a substantial research dissertation. The balance between theoretical understanding and practical implementation is a defining feature of this pathway — students do not just learn concepts, they build systems.

---

## What You Will Study — Year by Year

### Year 1 — Computing Foundations
Students establish core technical literacy across six modules:

- **Programming Concepts and Algorithms** — Python, algorithmic thinking, data structures, recursion, version control (Git)
- **Computer Systems** — CPU architecture, memory hierarchies, networking, operating systems, Linux fundamentals
- **Mathematical Skills for Computational Professionals** — Logic, set theory, graph theory, algorithmic complexity (Big O notation), RSA cryptography foundations
- **Programming Professional Practice** — Object-oriented programming, design patterns, memory management, unit testing, professional and ethical standards
- **Working with Data** — Database management, SQL, relational databases, NoSQL (MongoDB), data modelling, Big Data concepts
- **Integrative Project** — Team-based software development project integrating all Year 1 learning

### Year 2 — Core Computer Science and Introduction to AI
- **Software Engineering** — Requirements engineering, software design, testing, project management, documentation
- **Theory of Computation** — Automata theory, Turing machines, formal languages, computational complexity
- **Advanced Algorithms** — Trees, graphs, heaps, dynamic programming, divide and conquer, complexity analysis
- **Operating Systems, Security and Networks** — OS functions, security mechanisms, virtualisation, firewalls
- **Data Science** — Data wrangling, exploratory analysis, visualisation, predictive analytics, probability and statistics, Big Data tools (Hadoop, PySpark)
- **Artificial Intelligence** — Machine learning basics, search algorithms, NLP foundations, AI ethics, computer vision (TensorFlow, PyTorch, OpenCV)

### Year 3 — Advanced AI Specialization
- **Project Discovery** — Research methodology, literature review, project planning, ethical considerations
- **Machine Learning** — Linear and logistic regression, support vector machines, decision trees, unsupervised learning, model evaluation, bias-variance tradeoff, ethics in ML
- **Robotics and Intelligent Agents** — Search algorithms, knowledge representation, planning, probabilistic reasoning, decision-making under uncertainty
- **Artificial Neural Networks** — Neural network design and implementation, deep learning, CNNs, RNNs, applications in vision, speech, and robotics
- **Security** — Cryptography, infrastructure security, secure programming, PKI, digital signatures
- **Dissertation and Project Artifact** — Independent research project culminating in a technical artifact and written dissertation

## Career Opportunities

Graduates of this pathway are prepared for roles including:

- **AI Software Developer** — building intelligent applications and systems
- **Data Scientist** — analysing large datasets to extract insights and build predictive models
- **Machine Learning Engineer** — designing and deploying ML models in production systems
- **AI Researcher** — conducting research in academic or industry settings
- **Software Engineer** — general software development with AI specialization
- **System Architect** — designing large-scale intelligent systems
- **Computer Vision Engineer** — building systems that interpret visual data
- **NLP Engineer** — building systems that process and understand human language

## Who This Pathway Suits

This pathway is best suited to students who:

- Enjoy programming and find it comes naturally
- Are comfortable with mathematical reasoning and logic
- Are curious about how intelligent systems work — recommendation engines, language models, image recognition
- Enjoy working with data — finding patterns, building models, drawing conclusions
- Are interested in the cutting edge of technology — machine learning and AI are among the fastest-moving fields in computing
- Prefer building systems that learn and adapt rather than systems with fixed, explicit rules

Strong programming ability and genuine interest in AI concepts are the two strongest indicators of success in this pathway. Mathematical foundations, particularly linear algebra and statistics, become increasingly important in Year 2 and Year 3.

---

## How This Pathway Differs from the Others

Compared to Computing/Software Engineering, this pathway goes significantly deeper into mathematics, data, and intelligent systems — at the cost of breadth in areas like mobile development, UX design, and web APIs.

Compared to Cybersecurity, this pathway focuses on building and understanding systems rather than protecting and attacking them. The security content covered is foundational rather than specialist.

Students who choose AI are investing in a technically demanding, mathematically grounded specialization with strong industry demand and diverse career pathways.
